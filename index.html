At EXE2APK, we believe in rewarding our investors and community participants for their trust and commitment. That’s why we’ve implemented an innovative quarterly proportional dividend system that allows you to earn based on the number of tokens you hold and the length of time you hold them.



How Do Quarterly Dividends Work?

Every three months, we calculate our company’s net profit. This profit is distributed among all EXE2APK holders as dividends in USDT or USDC, depending on Average Holding using Discrete Sum Approximation and the profits generated during the quarter.


What is Average Holding using Discrete Sum Approximation?

To ensure a fair distribution, we use a mathematical concept called the "discrete sum approximation" to calculate the average holding of your tokens over the 90 days of a quarter. This method is derived from the integral calculus concept, specifically the mean value theorem for integrals, adapted to discrete intervals.


In continuous mathematics, the mean value theorem for integrals helps us find the average value of a function over a given interval. When dealing with discrete data points, like daily token balances, we use the sum of the product of the balance and the duration each balance was held, divided by the total number of days.
